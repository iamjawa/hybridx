# HybridX Breeder Glossary

## Seed Parent
The plant that produces the seeds (the female parent). In a cross, this is the plant whose flowers are pollinated.

## Pollen Parent
The plant that provides the pollen (the male parent). In a cross, this is the plant whose pollen is used to fertilise the seed parent.

## Cross
A planned breeding between two parent plants. The goal is to combine desirable traits from both parents.

## Hybrid
The offspring of a cross between two genetically distinct parents.

## F1, F2, F3
Generation labels. F1 is the first generation from a cross. F2 is the second generation (F1 × F1). Each subsequent generation is selected for desirable traits.

## Seed Batch
A group of seeds collected from one pollination event. Tracked through harvest, cleaning, storage, stratification, and germination.

## Stratification
A cold or warm treatment applied to seeds to break dormancy and encourage germination.

## Germination Rate
The percentage of seeds that successfully sprout. A key measure of seed batch quality.

## Seedling
A young plant grown from a seed in a breeding programme. Seedlings are evaluated to select the best individuals.

## Evaluation
A structured assessment of a seedling's traits. Scores are recorded for characteristics like colour, form, fragrance, and disease resistance.

## Disposition
The breeding decision for a seedling:
- **Selected** — Outstanding. Will be used as a future breeding parent.
- **Kept** — Good characteristics. Worth monitoring further.
- **Culled** — Does not meet objectives. Removed from the programme.
- **Sold/Gifted** — Distributed to other growers.
- **Dead** — Lost.

## Trait
A measurable characteristic of a plant (colour, fragrance, disease resistance, etc.). Traits are scored during evaluation.

## Breeding Goal
A defined objective with weighted criteria. Goals help you identify which plants and seedlings best match your breeding objectives.

## Pedigree
The recorded ancestry of a plant, showing parent plants and offspring across generations.

## Cross Planner
A tool that compares two parent plants side by side, showing trait overlap and compatibility.

## Quick Evaluation Mode
A keyboard-driven rapid scoring interface for evaluating many seedlings quickly. Type numbers to score, Tab to move between fields, Enter to save.
